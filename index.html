What does it look like?

The AES key is a byte array (the same "bytes" that a text file or an exe has, but isn't actually readable text). To actually convert it to readable text, most people will convert it into a hex string, or a hexadecimal representation of all the bytes. A hex string is a 64 character blob of text that only contains the numbers 0-9 and the letters a-f, sometimes with a "0x" behind it to show that it is a hex-encoded byte array.

So, what's it used for?

In order for leakers, 3d artists, and other content creators to grab the in-game assets efficiently, an "AES key" is required. There are methods of accessing the assets without an AES key, but it's impossible to get the name of a given file, or get information on anything other than cosmetics or images. Getting things like meshes, skeletons, loot tables, other general information, etc. isn't realistically feasable without an AES key.

How do you get it?

There are a few people in the community who know the method to getting the AES key by themselves. However, none of them are going to give you the method for fear of Epic Games patching their method(s).

How can I get it?

Some of those who have found a method share the AES key for a new release as soon as they get it. Some share it on Twitter or through an "API" or URL.
